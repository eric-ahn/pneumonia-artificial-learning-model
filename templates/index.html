About

From Conceiving to Deploying

  • August 2018

    Our Humble Beginnings

    MyPALM was first created when our a group of friends decided that it was time for a change.

  • December 2018

    A Team is Made

    MyPALM started in the production phase. The team started collecting massive amounts of data (around 6000 images) and started the training of their model: “PALM” This model is now the most advanced model in the world and actually stands for Pneumonia Artificial Learning Model.

  • March 2019

    Transition to Full Service

    The team decided it was time to move away from creating the model and move on to actually helping people with it. They went to local clinics to show them this model and asked how they can use it to help people in need.

  • by 2020

    To The Future

    The team hopes that before 2020 that this model will at least be used within the county that they reside. Although this may seem like a small step, it is one of many that will lead to revolutionizing the world.

Frequently Asked Questions Team Diagnosis Future

MyPALM is an advanced application created by our team. It has the ability to take images of chest x-rays and from there discern where or not a patient has pneumonia using Machine Learning.

MyPALM is based off PALMnet, our secret Neural Network through which every images passses through. It is extremely accurate and uses convolutional Neural Networks. It also has all the power of Google's Resnetv50, since PALMnet was created using transfer learning with Resnet as the base model.

The creators of MyPALM are actually three students from Lambert High School. The first is Milind Chandramohan, the student responsible for creating this website. The second is Akshat Gautam, the one that created the legendary PALMnet Model. The third is Eric Ahn, who helped create the PALMnet Model and documentation.

MyPALM started from unfortunate circumstances. Milind's aunt died from pneumonia which caused him to think about how someone could die from such a common and treatable disease. This caused Milind to to reach out to Akshat and Eric. This is when they worked together to create MyPALM.

All three hope to study computer science in college and use this knowledge to help their community, similarly to what they did here.

First, click the Browse button and choose your x-ray image. Afterwards, click Predict. This will then give you a PALM score, and a simple message about the predicted outcome.

The PALM score represents the confidence on which PALMnet believes the patient has pneumonia. A higher score means a higher chance of the patient having pneumonia and vice versa.

While MyPALM is very accurate, it is by no means a substitute to a doctor. If you have serious concerns whether you have pneumonia or any disease such as influenza that causes pneumonia, please call your physician immediately.

For immediate medical assitance, call 911. If you have any questions about pneumonia, you can call the American Lung Association at (800)586-4872.
